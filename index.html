Robert Oppenheimer

The Man Who Changed History

Robert Oppenheimer was a very smart scientist. He was born in New York in 1904. As a child, he loved learning and was very good at school.

He studied at famous universities at Harvard. Later, he became a teacher and helped many students become great scientists.

During World War II, he led a secret project to build a very powerful weapon called the atomic bomb.

"Now I am become Death, the destroyer of worlds."

— Oppenheimer's famous words after seeing the first atomic bomb test

Important Dates

  • 1904: Born in New York
  • 1925: Finished Harvard University very quickly
  • 1942: Started working on the atomic bomb project
  • July 1945: First atomic bomb test in New Mexico
  • August 1945: Bombs dropped on Japan
  • 1967: Died in Princeton, New Jersey

The Atomic Bomb Project

During the war, the U.S. government asked Oppenheimer to lead a team of scientists. Their job was to build a new kind of bomb using atomic energy.

They worked in secret in a place called Los Alamos. In 1945, they tested the first bomb. It was much more powerful than anyone expected.

Later that year, two atomic bombs were dropped on Japan. This helped end World War II, but many people died.

Why Oppenheimer is Important

  • Science: He helped make America a leader in physics
  • War: His work changed how wars are fought
  • Peace: Later, he wanted countries to control atomic weapons
  • Movies: His life inspired many films and books

After the War

After the war, Oppenheimer became famous but also had problems. Some people didn't trust him because of his old friends.

In 1954, the government took away his security job. But he kept teaching and thinking about science until he died.
